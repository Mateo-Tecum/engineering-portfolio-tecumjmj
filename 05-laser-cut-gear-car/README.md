# Laser-Cut Gear Car

## Overview

This project involved designing and building a laser-cut mechanical car using CAD-designed components and a gear-based drivetrain.

The goal was to apply mechanical design principles, gear system design, fabrication planning, and physical testing to create a functional small-scale vehicle.

## Key Features

- CAD-designed chassis and mechanical components
- Laser-cut structural parts
- Gear-based drivetrain
- Physical assembly and testing
- Iterative design and troubleshooting
- Performance evaluation based on movement and reliability

## Engineering Skills Used

- SolidWorks
- CAD modeling
- Gear systems
- Laser cutting
- Mechanical design
- Fabrication
- Assembly
- Testing and debugging

## Design and Build Process

The project began with CAD modeling of the car body, drivetrain layout, and gear components. Parts were then prepared for laser cutting, assembled into a physical prototype, and tested for motion. The design required consideration of gear alignment, structural stability, friction, and manufacturability.

## Results

The final car demonstrated mechanical motion using a gear-based system and laser-cut components. The project strengthened my ability to move from CAD design to fabricated hardware and evaluate how design decisions affect physical performance.
