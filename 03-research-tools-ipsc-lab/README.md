# Research Tools for iPSC Lab Work

## Overview

This project documents technical and engineering support work completed in an NIH-funded research environment involving induced pluripotent stem cell (iPSC) workflows.

My work included supporting lab equipment, assisting with microinjector-related tasks, contributing to cell culture workflows, thawing iPSCs, and designing/printing a data logger enclosure.

## Key Features

- Research equipment support
- Microinjector-related technical work
- iPSC thawing and lab workflow support
- 3D-printed data logger enclosure
- CAD-based research tooling
- Technical documentation and poster presentation

## Engineering Skills Used

- CAD

- Research tooling
- Lab equipment support
- Technical troubleshooting
- Biomedical research exposure
- Experimental workflow support

## Design and Build Process

The research work involved supporting a graduate student and lab team in maintaining and improving experimental workflows. Engineering-related contributions included equipment support, prototyping, and designing a 3D-printed enclosure for a data logger. The work required careful documentation, communication, and reliability in a biomedical research setting.

## Results

The project contributed to ongoing research operations by supporting experimental workflows and creating practical lab tooling. I also presented the work professionally through a research poster.
