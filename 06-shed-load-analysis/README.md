# Shed Load Analysis

## Overview

This project involved analyzing the structural loading of a 10-foot shed and evaluating whether the design could safely support expected loads.

The goal was to apply mechanics of materials concepts to a practical structural design problem, including load estimation, stress analysis, and safety evaluation.

## Key Features

- Structural load estimation
- Force and stress calculations
- Safety factor evaluation
- Mechanics of materials analysis
- Engineering report documentation
- Practical application to a small structure

## Engineering Skills Used

- Mechanics of materials
- Structural analysis
- Load calculations
- Stress and strain concepts
- Safety factor reasoning
- Technical reporting
- Engineering judgment

## Design and Analysis Process

The analysis began by identifying expected loads on the shed structure, including relevant distributed and concentrated loads. Calculations were then used to estimate internal stresses and determine whether the structure could safely support the expected loading conditions.

## Results

The final report evaluated the shed structure based on calculated loads, stress levels, and safety considerations. The project demonstrated how mechanics of materials concepts can be applied to real-world structural design and safety problems.
